Is there a racial bias in civilian complaints?

Composition by officer ethnicity:

The majority of complaints involve White officers.
As of 2010, 53% of the entire 34,526-member police force were white and 47% were members of minority groups.
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department#Demographics)

Composition by complainant ethnicity:

Half of the civilians who file complaints are Black. As of the 2010 census, the black population in NY is 25%.
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographic_history_of_New_York_City#Citywide

Are there officers with repeat complaints against them?

It is a little shocking how many officers have repeat complaints against them and, but more shocking is the amount of complaints against a given officer. These complaints go back to 1985.

How many were there in 2019?

There were 1,642 complaints in 2019 from 532 officers. There are currently 38,421 sworn officers in the NYPD
(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City_Police_Department)
This equates to 1.38% of the NYPD officers who have had complaints filed against them in 2019.
