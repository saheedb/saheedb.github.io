SR-20
Sunshade
SUNROOF/T-TOP/CONVERTIBLE TOP (SUNROOF)
8.Sunshade
A: REMOVAL
1) Disconnect the ground cable from battery. <Ref. to NT-5, BATTERY, NOTE, Note.>
NOTE:
For models other than STI model, disconnect the ground terminal from battery sensor.
2) Remove the lid assembly - sunroof. <Ref. to SR-7, REMOVAL, Glass Lid.>
3) Remove the trim panel - roof assembly. <Ref. to EI-99, REMOVAL, Roof Trim.>
4) Remove the sunshade assembly.
CAUTION:
Be careful not to damage the sunshade assembly.
(1) Attach protective tape (a) on the handle of sunshade assembly.
(2) Remove the left and right stoppers (b).
(3) Remove the sunshade assembly while pulling the center of the brace downward.
B: INSTALLATION
1) Install the sunshade assembly.
2) Install the trim panel - roof assembly. <Ref. to EI-102, INSTALLATION, Roof Trim.>
3) Install the lid assembly - sunroof. <Ref. to SR-7, REMOVAL, Glass Lid.>
4) Connect the battery ground terminal. <Ref. to NT-5, BATTERY, NOTE, Note.>
NOTE:
For models other than STI model, connect the ground terminal to battery sensor.

C: INSPECTION
1. MOVING LOAD OF SUNSHADE ASSEMBLY
1) Attach a spring scale together with a cloth to the edge of sunshade assembly.
2) Pull the spring scale to check the moving load of the sunshade assembly.
Moving load of sunshade assembly:
14
±
6 N (1.43 kgf, 3.1 lbf
±
0.61 kgf, 1.3 lbf)
NOTE:
Moving load is larger at the beginning of pulling a spring scale, so take a spring scale reading while the sun-
shade assembly is sliding smoothly.
3) If the inspection result exceeds the standard, check that the sunshade assembly or the rail assembly -
sunroof is installed properly. If necessary, replace the sunshade assembly or the rail assembly - sunroof.
